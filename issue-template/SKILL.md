# Issue Guide

**Keep in mind.** You can step back and decide not to do it at all at any step.

## Describe

The situation.

- Symptoms/logs/requests/observations.
- How big a problem is that?

### how to

- Title filled
- Description filled
- Description size is one paragraph summary
- I'm not saying what the problem is.
- I've mark it as feature or as problem.
- I've set the expected problem size as estimate.

> Title: I have a regular headache and can't work >5h.
> Describe: I have a regular headache. So, I can work only 5h a day. Mike said - smoking is the problem.
>
> or it's bigger problem
>
> Title: Headache daily causing employees work less.
> Describe: I have a regular headache. So, I can work only 5h a day. Oleg have a same problem. Mike said - smoking is the problem, but Oleg not smoking.

## Problem

Succinct statement of **unmet** user objectives and cause(s).

- What can be a problem?
- What is the short precise way we gona talk about the problem?

### how to

- I've listed possible problems / hypotheses.
- I've make the problem space smaller and picked one.

> Title: I think the new chair might be a good idea against the headache.
> Problem: bad glasses, bad chair.

- Problem size is one paragraph summary

> Title: I like Mike's chair. It helps him against the headache.
> Problem: +I have an uncomfortable chair.

## Approach

Approach Decision matrix (DM) to address the problem and best decision (best scope).

### how to

- I've list user intentions or objectives **in more detail** (wishlist)
- This is approaches I wona take. Buy or build? Lib or app? Modify or add? Automatic or manual?
- And tradeoffs of each. Be selective, it should sticks out and be matter to our problem.
- I have best of all in the title.

> Title: Buy a chair like Mike's when u have a headache.
> Approach:
>
> | Wish \\ Approach                                         | Buy it                 | Stole it                        | How |
> | -------------------------------------------------------- | ---------------------- | ------------------------------- | --- |
> | I wish to have a chair like Mike's against the headache. | (~) we need the budget | (-) Mike gona have same problem | -   |

## Design

This is how we accomplish users intentions. More Design Decision matrix's (DM's). Design = Implementation.

### how to

- I've made a plan with Design DM's.
- I've added "How" to taken approach.
- I've set the "solution + problem" to the title.

> Design:
>
> 1\. Find out Mike's chair id.
>
> 2\. Lookup the store.
>
> 3\. Check the cost.
>
> 4\. Agree on budget.
>
> 5\. Adjust chair pick if needed and it still comfortable or lookup diff stores for lower price.
>
> 6\. Buy it.
>
> Approach:
>
> | Wish \\ Approach                                         | …   | How                            |
> | -------------------------------------------------------- | --- | ------------------------------ |
> | I wish to have a chair like Mike's against the headache. | …   | Buy chair model XYZ on Alegro. |
>
> Title: Buy chair model XYZ on Alegro when u have a headache.

## Assessment

> Was it effective or ineffective? What went wrong? What went right? What can be improved? Lessons learned.
